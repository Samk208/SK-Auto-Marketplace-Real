# The $5,000 Question

"How much will it _actually_ cost to get this car to my driveway?"

This is the most common question we get. A car listed for $8,000 in Korea does not cost $8,000 in Lagos. Depending on your country, the final landed cost can be double that.

Let's break down the math using a real-world example: A **2019 Hyundai Tucson**.

---

# The Cost Breakdown

### 1. The FOB Price (Free On Board)

This is the price of the car itself, plus the cost to get it to the Korean port.

- **Car Price:** $9,500
- **Korea Local Logistics:** Included in SK AutoSphere pricing.
- **Total FOB:** **$9,500**

### 2. Ocean Freight (Shipping)

This is the ticket for the boat ride. Prices fluctuate with oil prices.

- **Destinations:** Lagos (Apapa/Tin Can), Mombasa, Tema, Dar es Salaam.
- **Average Cost (SUV):** **$1,300 - $1,600**
- **Insurance:** Optional but recommended (~$150).

### 3. Import Duty & Clearing (The Big One)

# How to Reduce Your Costs

You can legally lower your import bill by being smart:

1.  **Buy Older Models:** In Ghana and Nigeria, slightly older cars (within the allowed age limit) often attract lower valuations.
2.  **Ship RORO:** Container shipping protects the car better but costs $1,000+ more. RORO is safe and standard.
3.  **Buy in Bulk:** If you are a dealer, shipping multiple cars in one container can reduce the per-unit freight cost.
4.  **Use SK AutoSphere:** We have negotiated rates with major shipping lines (Eukor, Glovis) that are often lower than public rates.
