# Step-by-Step Import Process

Importing a car might seem daunting, but it follows a logical 5-step process.

### Step 1: Verification & Purchase

Before spending a dollar, verify the vehicle.

- **Don't rely on photos alone.** Photos can be photoshopped.
- **Use Video Inspection.** Demand a live video walk-around.
- **Check the VIN.** Run a check to see accident history.

**Pro Tip:** SK AutoSphere uses AI to scan every vehicle, flagging dents and scratches that the human eye might miss.

### Step 2: Documentation Checklist

To clear customs smoothly, you will need a specific set of documents. We auto-generate these for you:

- **Bill of Lading (BL):** Proof of ownership.
- **Commercial Invoice:** Used to calculate duty.
- **Export Certificate:** Shows the car was legally exported.
- **Inspection Certificate:** Required by some countries (like Kenya's QISJ).

### Step 3: Shipping (RORO vs. Container)

You have two main choices getting your car across the ocean:

| Method                      | Cost                     | Speed    | Safety    | Best For                      |
| :-------------------------- | :----------------------- | :------- | :-------- | :---------------------------- |
| **RORO (Roll-on/Roll-off)** | Lowest ($1,000 - $1,500) | Moderate | Good      | Standard cars, SUVs           |
| **Container**               | Higher ($2,500+)         | Fast     | Excellent | Luxury cars, Multi-car orders |

Most buyers choose **RORO** for its balance of cost and efficiency.

# Avoiding Common Scams

The internet is full of "too good to be true" deals. Here is how to stay safe:

1.  **The "Ghost Car" Scam:** A seller lists a beautiful car at half price, takes your deposit, and disappears.
    - _Solution:_ Only pay to verified company corporate accounts, never personal Western Union.
2.  **The "Bait and Switch":** You buy a clean car in photos, but receive a wrecked one.
    - _Solution:_ Use an Escrow service (like SK AutoSphere's) that holds funds until the car is verified at the port.
